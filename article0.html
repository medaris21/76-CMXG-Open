Retired Pro Wins Gold In His New Sport!

Published 3 Hours Ago by CMXG Press

From Fairways to Fast Lanes: Benjamin Case Sprints Into a New Era, Hair First

Just months after his dramatic and oddly medical retirement from professional golf, Benjamin Case has reemerged—not on the green, but on the track.

The former CMXG phenom, whose “severely sprained liver” during a Chili’s incident shocked the sports world, is now turning heads (and turning laps) as an up-and-coming sprinter.

“Golf was my past,” Case said while lacing up a pair of neon-orange spikes.

“But now, it’s all about speed, freedom, and letting the wind flow through this glorious mane.”

Indeed, Case’s transition to track has been marked not just by personal bests, but by personal growth, particularly in the follicular department.

Gone is the clean-cut country club look.

In its place: shoulder-length waves that flutter heroically behind him as he crosses finish lines and turns casual practice jogs into shampoo commercial-worthy slow-motion sequences.

“It’s like if Tarzan joined a relay team,” one teammate noted.

“Except faster. And somehow smoother.”

Case has already qualified for regionals in the 200m dash, though sources close to him say he’s lobbying for a new event: the 4x100 “Freedom Flow” relay, in which baton passes are judged not only by speed but also by hair flair.

Meanwhile, doctors confirm his liver is now “healing nicely,” with one stating, “Honestly, the cardiovascular activity seems to be doing wonders. Though we still recommend he avoid lifting heavy platters at chain restaurants.”

As for the CMXG golf world, many are still recovering from the Case-sized void.

“He may be gone from golf,” said a longtime fan, “but he’s out there setting records and shampoo standards the world over.”

Whether on the green or the track, Benjamin Case proves once again: greatness can’t be shaved down.

It grows wild, runs fast, and always finds a new finish line.
