#1 Ranked CMXG Pro Retires After Spraining Liver

Published 3 Hours Ago by CMXG Press

In a shocking turn of events that has sent ripples through the local golf community, top-ranked CMXG pro Benjamin Case announced his immediate retirement from competitive golf. The cause? A freak injury described by his agent as a "severely sprained liver," sustained during a pre-tournament celebratory dinner.

Witnesses report that Case, known for his powerful swing and even more powerful post-game celebrations, was attempting to demonstrate the "perfect follow-through" using a fully-loaded appetizer platter at a local Chili's. The unexpected torque, combined with what doctors are calling "extreme internal lubrication," led to the unprecedented medical diagnosis.

"We've seen sprained ankles, torn rotator cuffs, even the occasional case of the yips," said Dr. Alistair Finch, a sports medicine specialist. "But a sprained liver? This is one for the textbooks. It suggests a level of athletic—and perhaps social—commitment we rarely see."

Case's retirement leaves a power vacuum at the top of the CMXG rankings just weeks before the 76 CMXG Open. "It's anyone's game now," commented one rival golfer. "But mostly, we're just impressed. I didn't even know you could do that." The CMXG Open committee has wished Benjamin a speedy recovery and has already begun discussions on adding "liver safety" to the tournament's official rulebook.
